## A. "失败" / "失效" / "无效" — When does an event or pattern FAIL? What invalidates it?

### Book1 支撑失效 (Support Invalidations):

[SOURCE] book1 L810-817:
"判断支撑是否有效，主要看从支撑处起来的强度。假如你在支撑位进场买入，说明你假设了这里有大量买单流入而阻止了下跌。如果接下来的几天没有表现出持续的需求，反而是低量小幅波动，说明没有大量买单流入市场，这个支撑无效，我们应立刻出场等候。"
→ interpretation: Support invalidation rule: after touching support, if no sustained demand shows (low volume, small bars) → support is invalid → exit immediately.

[SOURCE] book1 L813-817:
"蜡烛3是供应继续主导市场的信号，上影线说明当日卖单流入量大于买单（供应战胜需求），同时成交量扩大，这说明反弹遇到了强大的供应（主力高抛所致）。结论是支撑无效，导致价格继续下跌。"
→ interpretation: Rebound with expanding volume + upper shadows = supply beating demand = support fails. If you bought the bounce and see this, "立刻出场，不要用自己的情绪和希望来否定市场本质行为"

### Book2 趋势线突破不等于反转:

[SOURCE] book2 L2421-2423:
"如果没有其他的证据证明趋势发生实质性变化，仅仅因为价格突破了供给线，或者突破了长期盘整区域就进行股票买入操作，这将是非常糟糕的...趋势线被打破本身并不是一定表明趋势已经发生反转，更不能代表一切。重要的是趋势线打破时的具体情况以及它是怎样被打破的。"
→ interpretation: A trendline break alone does NOT validate a trend change. Need confirming evidence from price-volume behavior at the break point. This is critical for not over-triggering chain breaks.

[SOURCE] book2 L2425:
"进入盘整之后，促成原来趋势的力量会被改变或者加强。只有形成原来趋势的力量变得更强才能使得原来的趋势恢复。"
→ interpretation: After consolidation, the original trend force must strengthen for the trend to resume — not just resume mechanically.

### Book3 突破失败 (Break/Spring Failure):

[SOURCE] book3 L262-264:
"不管边界何时被破坏，其决定性因素都在于买卖盘的跟进或者缺乏跟进。在突破或突破失败出现之后，价格通常会再次测试这些区域。"
→ interpretation: The decisive factor for ANY boundary break is FOLLOW-THROUGH (跟进) or lack thereof. After a breakout or failed breakout, price typically retests.

## B. "Spring" + "失败" — When does a Spring fail?

### Book1 Primary Spring Failure Definition:

[SOURCE] book1 L1192:
"成功的弹簧效应之后，价格会持续上涨。如果弹簧效应之后没有出现持续上涨的价格，说明需求乏力，这会使供应积极，并使价格下跌。如果在牛市中出现连续失败的弹簧效应，说明牛市已经乏力。下冲反弹之后，价格应该创新高，如果没有，说明需求力量不足，价格行为或者形成区间，或者进入熊市。"
→ interpretation: Spring fails when: (1) After the spring, price does NOT continue rising (no sustained demand). (2) Consecutive failed Springs in a bull market → bull is exhausted. (3) After spring, price must make new highs; failure to do so → either range formation or bear market.

[SOURCE] book1 L2623:
"如果没有达到牛市继续的目的，或者价格上涨半途而废，说明Spring行为失败，这个失败对牛市的结束有重大意义。"
→ interpretation: Spring failure = the spring didn't accomplish its purpose of continuing the bull trend. This failure has major significance for trend termination.

[SOURCE] book1 L2661-2662:
"还有一种是Spring失败。当我们确定突破形成第2种或者第3种Spring的时候，如果反弹没有出现价格持续上涨和成交量随价格上涨递增的情况，说明反弹失败。另外，这个无力的弹簧会导致价格突破支撑后继续下跌。这种情况经常发生在熊市中或者有强劲供应的背景中。"
→ interpretation: Even if spring structure looks good (type 2 or 3), the rebound must show: (a) sustained price increase; (b) increasing volume as price rises. Without these → spring fails → price continues below support. Common in bear markets or strong supply backgrounds.

[SOURCE] book1 L2676-2677:
"Spring之后，价格稳定上涨，说明需求保持强劲...Spring是否失败还在于它能否把价格带出震荡区。"
→ interpretation: Spring success also depends on whether it can lift price OUT of the trading range.

[SOURCE] book1 L2520:
"蜡烛6是个弹簧效应，足以看出抄底的很多。但是这个反弹因没有吸引到更多的需求（无量上涨）而失败。市场进行了再次测试，需求还是没有上来，再次说明牛市可能终结。"
→ interpretation: Spring + low-volume rally = spring failure → bull market may end. Confirmed by further tests showing no demand.

### Book3 Spring (探底回升) Failure:

[SOURCE] book3 L2153-2155:
"下跌趋势中失败的探底回升随处可见，称为'抄底者的噩梦'，它们反复一次次地引诱交易者追逐向上反转的行情。"
→ interpretation: Failed springs in a downtrend are extremely common — they are "bottom-fisher's nightmares." Each failed spring is a trap luring longs.

[SOURCE] book3 L2287:
"探底回升失败凸显空头实力的增长"
→ interpretation: Failed springs (探底回升失败) highlight growing bear strength. This is a chapter heading in Weis.

[SOURCE] book3 L2757-2761:
"多次探底回升失败，空头正在消化多头筹码"
→ interpretation: Multiple failed springs = bears are absorbing bull positions. When springs repeatedly fail at a large range bottom, bears are in control.

[SOURCE] book3 L1278-1279:
"我们把8b日看成是想移动到前支撑线上面的一次尝试。8b的收盘位置警示我们，这次尝试已经失败。"
→ interpretation: Closing position within the failed attempt is key: if the bar trying to reclaim support closes poorly, the attempt has failed.

## C. "SOS" + "失效" — When does an SOS become invalid?

[SOURCE] book1 L1521:
"SOS本身是需求控制市场的信号。它出现之后，价格会回来测试确认SOS（测试是否需求大于供应）。一旦测试出现了长阴伴随扩大的成交量，这个SOS立刻失效，价格会继续在震荡区波动，或者有更多的测试。"
→ interpretation: SOS invalidation: After SOS appears, price retests it. If the retest produces a LONG RED BAR with EXPANDING VOLUME → SOS is immediately invalidated → price returns to range or needs more tests.

[SOURCE] book1 L1592 (JOC as SOS variant):
"JOC必须发生在震荡区的右手边，而且突破后，成交量必须有跟随。即使有回调，也不能出现放量阴线的行为。否则，JOC失效，价格会再次回到震荡区。"
→ interpretation: JOC (a form of SOS) invalidation: (1) Must occur on the right side of the range. (2) Volume must FOLLOW through. (3) Pullbacks must NOT show expanding-volume red bars. Any violation → JOC fails → price returns to range.

## D. "SOW" + "有效" / "确认" / "失效" — When is SOW confirmed vs invalidated?

[SOURCE] book1 L1187:
"SOW：Sign of Weakness，显示供应控制市场。价格下降伴随变宽的振幅，增加至非常大的成交量。SOW之前常常是一个交易区间，它是熊市的标志。SOW需要随后的反弹确认，如果反弹无力，确认SOW有效。"
→ interpretation: SOW confirmation: SOW requires subsequent rebound for confirmation. If the rebound is WEAK/lacks force (low volume, small bars) → SOW is CONFIRMED VALID.

[SOURCE] book1 L2267-2269:
"SOW的特点是放量长阴，必须出现在震荡区的右手边才有效。当这种行为出现的时候，市场只是出现了可能的SOW，它必须要经过紧接着的无量上涨才能确认。"
→ interpretation: SOW must appear on RIGHT SIDE of range to be valid. SOW is only "potential" — must be confirmed by a subsequent LOW-VOLUME RALLY (无力反弹).

[SOURCE] book1 L2300-2301:
"SOW之后的确认过程如果是相对大的阳线伴随递增成交量，说明市场有新的需求进入接盘。如果这种现象出现，SOW的功能就失效。"
→ interpretation: SOW INVALIDATION: If after SOW, the confirmation rally shows RELATIVELY LARGE green bars with INCREASING VOLUME → new demand is entering → SOW is INVALIDATED. The pattern breaks!

[SOURCE] book1 L2520:
"接下来的SOW再次确认了供应的强劲。后面的无量回升确认了SOW的有效性。"
→ interpretation: Low-volume rally after SOW = SOW is confirmed valid → supply is strong.

## E. "吸筹" + "失败" / "终止" / "无效" — When does accumulation fail or get invalidated?

[SOURCE] book1 L715:
"他们失败的原因就是没有等到吸筹过程结束，这个过程就是因，后面产生的趋势就是果。"
→ interpretation: Traders fail by NOT waiting for accumulation to complete. Accumulation IS the cause, trend IS the effect. Jumping in before accumulation finishes = failure.

[SOURCE] book1 L1850-1852:
"反弹失效：从蜡烛14起来后，两次反弹都止于50%位置（12～14这波的50%），这属于正常回调，不是反转，说明市场还是熊市。这两次反弹的成交量没有减少，价格上涨却半路夭折，足以说明供应的压力很大，吸筹还没有最后确认。"
→ interpretation: During suspected accumulation, if rallies stop at 50% and volume doesn't decrease → supply pressure is still large → accumulation NOT confirmed yet. This can lead to shakeout; only a successful shakeout confirms accumulation.

[SOURCE] book1 L1302-1303:
"二次测试过程中价格波动和成交量与恐慌抛售相比都非常小，说明市场上的供应在减弱，这会导致熊市的停止，这种是成功的二次测试。否则，如果成交量在测试过程中继续扩大，说明市场上浮动供应还在，那样会导致价格突破支撑，然后熊市恢复。"
→ interpretation: Secondary test during accumulation: small volume + small range = successful test → bear market stops. Expanding volume on test → floating supply still exists → price breaks support → bear trend resumes → ACCUMULATION FAILS.

[SOURCE] book1 L1816:
"从2、3、4开始的下跌，告诉我们市场的浮动供应还大量存在，CM需要把这些浮动供应吸收后，才能允许价格上涨...这种在底部出现需求耗尽的情况，往往导致震仓或者继续下跌的行情，接下来如果供应继续扩大（价格回落如果是扩大的成交量），说明熊市会继续。"
→ interpretation: During accumulation, if demand exhausts at the bottom (demand exhaustion) → either shakeout or continued decline. If the decline shows EXPANDING volume → bear market continues → accumulation not confirmed/may fail.

## F. "派发" + "失败" / "终止" — When does distribution fail?

[SOURCE] book1 L2314-2318 (critical re-evaluation):
"从图3-9的分析，我们知道蜡烛5是SOW...如果蜡烛5恐慌抛售之后，我们从吸筹的角度看待当时的行情...那么我们从图3-9得出的派发结论会被市场自身行为推翻。"
→ interpretation: A distribution interpretation can be OVERTURNED by market behavior. What looks like SOW (distribution confirmation) could actually be panic selling → re-accumulation. The market itself can invalidate your distribution thesis.

[SOURCE] book1 L2318:
"如果出现SOS，终极震仓或弹簧效应，说明这个震荡区是再吸筹，然后牛市恢复。"
→ interpretation: Distribution scenario is INVALIDATED when: SOS appears, or terminal shakeout appears, or spring appears → these indicate re-accumulation, not distribution → bull resumes.

[SOURCE] book1 L2300-2301:
"SOW之后的确认过程如果是相对大的阳线伴随递增成交量，说明市场有新的需求进入接盘。如果这种现象出现，SOW的功能就失效。"
→ interpretation: SOW failure = distribution failure: strong demand entering after SOW invalidates the bearish signal.

## G. "跌破" + "支撑" / "区间" + "底" — Breaking below support

[SOURCE] book1 L1491-1492:
"终极震仓的表现形式是：深度跌破吸筹区间底部支撑，这是CM惯用的一个手段，目的是使公众因恐慌而清仓。这是吸筹结束的一个信号。"
→ interpretation: Deep break below accumulation range support = terminal shakeout = accumulation ENDING signal (bullish, not bearish). This is a CRITICAL distinction: breaking support during accumulation can be the FINAL shakeout, not invalidation!

[SOURCE] book2 L1365:
"如果下降趋势继续甚至跌破95点这个支撑位，有可能将迫使又一轮资金流出...如果股价跌破9月21日的低点94点，并且伴随量能放大，那么我们需要更进一步卖空。"
→ interpretation: Breaking prior low with AMPLIFYING volume → bearish continuation confirmed → more shorting warranted.

[SOURCE] book2 L1916:
"64点位的支撑失败——明显的熊市从跌破58或55开始，把股价拖离前期支撑的60~59，到达新低。"
→ interpretation: Support failure confirmed when price breaks below and DRAGS AWAY from prior support to NEW LOWS. The level must actually be violated.

[SOURCE] book2 L2331:
"如果指数跌破之前的支撑线，且成交量增至每天3000或4000手，我们就可以把这视作为一个相对大的变化，表明供应量的增加已经大于相对较小的需求量。"
→ interpretation: Support break with volume EXPANSION = supply overwhelming demand → bearish.

## H. "创新低" + "吸筹" / "积累" — New low during suspected accumulation

[SOURCE] book2 L1942:
"跌到46是为了使该股显出弱势的表象而故意制造的震荡，这样有利于内幕者吸筹，也能够在下一波主升浪的起始阶段打击多头的积极性，或许也是为了鼓励弱势公众空方的形成，来把股价打到新低。"
→ interpretation: New lows during accumulation can be DELIBERATE — a manufactured shakeout to make the stock appear weak so insiders can accumulate. This is BULLISH, not chain-breaking!

[SOURCE] book1 L1305:
"成功的二次测试可以告诉我们前面恐慌抛售所形成的支撑是安全的，一旦在后面的吸筹过程中出现急速跌破这个支撑，说明吸筹已经接近结束，因为那个急跌属于终极震仓，目的是扫清市场上最后一批浮动供应。"
→ interpretation: A sharp break below the panic selling support DURING accumulation = accumulation is NEARING COMPLETION (terminal shakeout). This is NOT a chain break — it's confirmation of accumulation!

[SOURCE] book2 L1940-1941:
"54的高点低于前高点55，46的低点也低于前低点48。这难道还不是一个向下的趋势吗？或许如此，但也可能与此相反..."
→ interpretation: Lower lows during suspected accumulation do NOT automatically invalidate accumulation. They may be shakeouts — must examine broader context.

[SOURCE] book3 L2139-2141:
"8月份，价格创新低，略低于前期低点。当道指在8月恢复到7月高点之上时，探底回升进入了高速期，把前11个月的所有高点抛在后面，宣告了难得一见的牛市的开始。"
→ interpretation: A new low that is SLIGHTLY below a prior low, followed by recovery above prior highs = spring/terminal shakeout that launches a massive bull market. New low ≠ chain break in accumulation context.

## I. "二次测试" + "失败" — When does a secondary test fail?

[SOURCE] book1 L1302-1303:
"如果二次测试过程中价格波动和成交量与恐慌抛售相比都非常小，说明市场上的供应在减弱，这会导致熊市的停止，这种是成功的二次测试。否则，如果成交量在测试过程中继续扩大，说明市场上浮动供应还在，那样会导致价格突破支撑，然后熊市恢复。"
→ interpretation: Secondary test FAILS when: volume EXPANDS during test → floating supply still exists → price breaks support → bear trend resumes. Secondary test SUCCEEDS when: small volume + small range → supply weakening → bear stops.

[SOURCE] book3 L5060-5065:
"测试(Test)：价格接近市场既定的支撑或阻力水平。如果股价在支撑位或阻力位内，则测试通过。然而，如果股价创新低或新高，则测试失败。"
→ interpretation: A test FAILS if price makes a new low (testing support) or new high (testing resistance) — the level is breached.

## J. "震仓" + "失败" — When does a shakeout fail?

No explicit "shakeout failure" found in any text. However:

[SOURCE] book1 L1852:
"这两次反弹的成交量没有减少，价格上涨却半路夭折，足以说明供应的压力很大，吸筹还没有最后确认。这种情况会导致震仓发生，只要再次震仓成功，就确认了市场在吸筹。"
→ interpretation: A shakeout that DOES happen and succeeds → confirms accumulation. Implicitly, if the shakeout doesn't occur or doesn't bounce back → accumulation not confirmed. The text treats shakeout as a test that can "succeed" or implicitly "need to be retried."

[SOURCE] book3 L2102-2106:
"1999年至2002年之间的价格是一次终结性震仓(Terminal Shakeout)...然而，从2002年低点开始的上涨，大部分都在2004年丧失，成了一次终结性震仓的二次测试。"
→ interpretation: Terminal shakeout can have secondary tests. If the secondary test after the shakeout FAILS (price loses most of the gain) → the shakeout didn't produce a sustained bull trend → essentially a failed shakeout.

## K. "反弹" + "一半" / "50%" / "百分比" — The 50% rule

### Book1 (explicit):

[SOURCE] book1 L1850-1852:
"从蜡烛14起来后，两次反弹都止于50%位置（12～14这波的50%），这属于正常回调，不是反转，说明市场还是熊市。"
→ interpretation: Rallies stopping at 50% retracement = NORMAL correction within bear trend, NOT reversal.

[SOURCE] book1 L2175:
"B～C的回调明显突破了A～B的50%（4621），而且价格波动和成交量有明显增长现象，这是供应扩大的现象，此时牛市受到威胁。"
→ interpretation: Breaking below 50% retracement with EXPANDING volume and range = supply expanding = bull market THREATENED (not yet killed, but warning).

[SOURCE] book1 L2150:
"如果连续几次的反弹都无法接近超买线，更说明需求已经很弱，后市如果出现供应扩大的现象（回落超过50%和放量突破支撑线），会导致派发出现。"
→ interpretation: Falling below 50% + volume break of support line → distribution begins.

[SOURCE] book1 L2175-2177:
"突破幅度减小和50%原则一起用，其中突破幅度递减表示市场的需求开始减少，而价格突破50%又说明供应在扩大...说明牛市暂时停止"
→ interpretation: SOT (shortening of thrust) + 50% violation → bull market temporarily stopped → distribution phase may begin.

[SOURCE] book1 L2185-2186:
"这个回调已经严重超过了前面上涨波的50%，说明需求在减弱...反弹没有超过前面下跌波的50%，这再一次确认了牛市的结束"
→ interpretation: 50% rule works in both directions: breaking below 50% of a rally = demand weakening; failing to reclaim 50% of a decline = supply still dominant.

### Book2 (50% principle):

[SOURCE] book2 L1245:
"正常的反弹应该在14点一半的位置下，即5点或6点...指数从低位反弹7点，正好是14点一半的位置。但成交量并没有达到2月上涨的标准量。这就意味着：(1)这纯粹是技术性反弹；(2)多方力量衰竭"
→ interpretation: Rally to 50% with INADEQUATE volume = technical/short-covering rally only → bull exhaustion → confirmed bear.

[SOURCE] book2 L2737-2738:
"按照'50%法则'(从高点下降上一个高点与上一个低点之差的一半)价位低一个点再结束我们的多头头寸。"
→ interpretation: 50% rule used as a stop-loss trigger: when price falls below 50% of the prior swing range → exit longs.

[SOURCE] book2 L2749:
"6月7日的下跌已经超过了我们'50%法则'(表明弱势)"
→ interpretation: Violating the 50% rule = indicates WEAKNESS.

## L. "Rule 4" / "规则4" / "Rule 5" / "规则5" — Wyckoff's specific rules

No explicit "Rule 4" or "Rule 5" found in any text. Wyckoff did not enumerate rules in this way in these texts. However, implicit rules about invalidation:

[SOURCE] book2 L2421:
"如果没有其他的证据证明趋势发生实质性变化，仅仅因为价格突破了供给线...就进行股票买入操作，这将是非常糟糕的"
→ interpretation: Implicit rule: NEVER trade a trendline break alone without confirming evidence from price-volume context.

[SOURCE] book1 L811:
"假如你在支撑位进场买入...如果接下来的几天没有表现出持续的需求，反而是低量小幅波动，说明没有大量买单流入市场，这个支撑无效，我们应立刻出场等候。"
→ interpretation: Implicit rule: If support doesn't generate follow-through demand, support is INVALID → exit immediately.

## M. "恢复" + "熊市" / "恢复下跌" — Rally failure and bear resumption

[SOURCE] book1 L1302-1303 (key passage):
"如果二次测试过程中...成交量在测试过程中继续扩大，说明市场上浮动供应还在，那样会导致价格突破支撑，然后熊市恢复。"
→ interpretation: Bear trend RESUMPTION after a rally happens when: secondary test shows EXPANDING volume → floating supply persists → support breaks → bear resumes.

## SUMMARY: Key Chain-Break Rules from Original Texts

### MUST-BREAK Conditions (Chain should BREAK):
1. SOS test: long red bar + expanding volume → SOS invalidated (book1 L1521)
2. JOC: pullback with expanding-volume red bars → JOC invalidated (book1 L1592)
3. SOW invalidation: recovery with large green bars + increasing volume → SOW invalid → distribution thesis overturned (book1 L2300-2301)
4. Spring failure: no sustained rally after spring + no increasing volume → spring failed (book1 L1192, L2661)
5. Failed secondary test: expanding volume on test → support breaks → bear resumes (book1 L1302-1303)
6. 50% rule violation: break below 50% retracement + expanding volume → bull threatened/distribution (book1 L2175)
7. Support invalidation: touch support, no sustained demand → support invalid (book1 L811)
8. Spring failure to exit range → spring fails (book1 L2676-2677)

### MUST-NOT-BREAK Conditions (Chain should SURVIVE):
1. New low during accumulation = potential terminal shakeout → BULLISH, not bearish (book1 L1491, L1305; book2 L1942)
2. Break below support with small volume during accumulation = likely spring, not invalidation (book1 L1492)
3. Secondary test with SMALL volume + small range = successful = accumulation valid (book1 L1302)
4. Trendline break alone ≠ trend change (book2 L2421-2423)
5. Distribution scenario can be overturned by SOS/terminal shakeout/spring → re-accumulation (book1 L2314-2318)
6. Spring slightly below range with volume that doesn't crush demand → wait for secondary test (book1 L2657)

### Nuanced / Context-Dependent:
1. Shakeout failure: not explicitly discussed but can be implied from secondary test failure (book3 L2102-2106)
2. Multiple failed springs = bear power growing, even from range bottom (book3 L2287, L2757-2761)
3. Failed rally to 50% in bear market = normal correction, not reversal (book1 L1852; book2 L1245)
4. The 50% rule + SOT together = strong bearish confirmation (book1 L2175-2177)
